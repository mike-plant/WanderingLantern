## Make Something. Perform It.

Spend a week of summer evenings laughing, creating, and performing. Campers will play improv games, explore puppetry using simple objects, and work together to create a playful end-of-week performance.

We'll use the open space at **Wagar Park** to be loud, silly, and expressive — then head into **The Wandering Lantern** to focus on character, storytelling, and performance.

Led by **Melissa Vincel** and **Fé Beatty**, this camp helps kids build confidence, creativity, and connection in a warm, supportive setting.

### Dates & Times

**June 8–12, 2026 · 5:30 PM – 8:30 PM each evening**

### Details

- Limited to **20 campers** with 2 instructors
- Held at **The Wandering Lantern** and **Wagar Park**
- Bring dinner and a water bottle each evening
- Drop-off and pick-up on **Park Place** at the lower field of Wagar Park
- **End-of-week showcase** Friday, June 12 from 8:00–8:30 PM at The Wandering Lantern

### About the Instructors

**Melissa Vincel** is a Lakewood-based writer, editor, and teaching artist who works with young writers and directs the Theater Club at Grant Elementary. She loves helping kids build confidence, community, and connection through creative play.

**Fé Beatty** is a storyteller, composer, playwright, and performer studying Composition and English at Ohio State, with minors in Dance and Theatre. They bring imagination, playfulness, and a love of collaboration to every creative space.
